# Academic Learning Model — Keystone Prep High School

## Philosophy

At Keystone Prep High School, we believe an interdisciplinary approach creates exciting learning opportunities that ensure all students become active participants in our project-based educational model. This includes scientific dissections, rocketry experiments, archaeological digs, analyzing artifact remains and environmental components, exploring styles of art, and applying real-world mathematical concepts. Students learn how to apply knowledge situationally and reflect on the world around them.

---

## Core Teaching Approaches

### Project-Based Learning (PBL)

Project-based learning is an educational approach where students learn by actively engaging in real-world, complex projects that often involve problem-solving and collaboration.

- **Duration:** 18-week time frame (long-term)
- Students look at a variety of solutions from different disciplines to include in their project
- May require community collaborations
- Students participate in the solution
- **Skills:** Collaboration, communication, self-direction
- Student projects may make a real-world impact
- Involves authentic learning experiences

---

### Problem-Based Learning

Problem-based learning is an educational approach where students learn by actively engaging with real-world, complex problems that are short-term in duration and small in scope. Instead of passively receiving information, students work in groups to investigate the problem, analyze information, and develop solutions.

- **Duration:** 1–5 day time frame (short-term)
- Students look at a variety of solutions but focus on a few
- Does not usually require community collaborations
- Solutions may be theoretical or applied
- **Skills:** Perspective-taking, teamwork, information literacy
- Student work is isolated to the classroom

---

### What Both Approaches Share

- Real-world problems
- Interdisciplinary learning
- Heavy emphasis on skill-building:
  - Problem solving
  - Critical thinking
  - Introspection
  - Creativity

---

### Interdisciplinary Projects

Interdisciplinary projects integrate knowledge and perspectives from two or more academic disciplines to address a complex issue or solve a problem. These projects encourage collaboration, innovation, and a deeper understanding of how different fields relate to one another. They are woven into both Problem-Based and Project-Based learning models.

---

### Thematic Curriculum

A thematic curriculum organizes learning around a central theme, integrating various subjects and activities to create a more cohesive and engaging educational experience. At KPHS, our three magnet programs serve as the thematic backbone:

- **CSI: Odessa** — Crime Scene Investigation
- **Mission Control: Odessa** — Space & STEM
- **Visual Art & Design: Odessa** — Fine Arts

---

### Portfolio Assessments

Portfolio assessment is a method of evaluating student learning by gathering and assessing a collection of their work over time, showcasing their progress and achievements. It provides a comprehensive view of a student's learning journey as an alternative to traditional exams.

---

## Field Trips

As part of our Multi-Track Magnet Program, discipline-specific field trips are offered for all grade levels once a semester. Trips are:

- Age-appropriate
- Curriculum-integrated (all students can participate)
- Varied by course and grade level

See individual magnet program files for program-specific field trip destinations.
