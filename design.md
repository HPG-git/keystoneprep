# Brand & Style

The Keystone Prep visual system should feel like a supportive environment where students are known, encouraged, and prepared for their future.

The visual identity should communicate:

- Personalized support
- Confidence and growth
- Student-centered learning
- Warmth and trust
- Future readiness
- Hands-on engagement

The aesthetic can be described as:

**Modern Human-Centered Education**

Rather than feeling institutional or elite, the experience should feel approachable, inspiring, and optimistic.

---

# Colors

The color system reflects both trust and growth.

### Primary Blue

Represents:

- Stability
- Trust
- Guidance
- Academic confidence

Use for:

- Navigation
- Primary buttons
- Headlines
- Major actions

---

### Secondary Green

Represents:

- Growth
- Progress
- Support
- Student development

Use for:

- Supporting callouts
- Student success indicators
- Learning highlights

---

### Accent Orange

Represents:

- Curiosity
- Energy
- Creativity
- Student engagement

Use sparingly:

- Highlight moments
- CTA emphasis
- Program accents

---

### Background Colors

Use light neutral surfaces to create openness and breathing room.

Avoid harsh white or dark institutional colors.

---

# Typography

Typography should feel modern, clear, and approachable.

### Headlines

Use:

Poppins

Characteristics:

- Friendly
- Confident
- Contemporary
- Student-focused

---

### Body Copy

Use:

Inter

Characteristics:

- Highly readable
- Functional
- Clean

---

# Layout Philosophy

Use a flexible editorial layout that feels open and welcoming.

Desktop:

- 12-column responsive grid
- Max width: 1280px
- Large whitespace between sections

Mobile:

- 4-column layout
- Prioritize vertical scanning
- Large tap targets

---

# Spacing Rhythm

Use an 8px system.

Prefer larger spacing values throughout the experience.

The site should feel calm and uncluttered.

---

# Elevation & Depth

Depth should feel soft and natural.

Use:

- subtle shadows
- soft tonal layering
- rounded cards

Avoid:

- harsh shadows
- heavy borders
- sharp separations

---

# Photography Style

Photography is critical.

Use authentic imagery showing:

- students collaborating
- teachers interacting with students
- hands-on learning
- projects and presentations
- field experiences
- student confidence moments

Avoid:

- stock graduation photos
- posed classroom images
- institutional imagery

Photography should feel:

- candid
- optimistic
- human
- active

---

# Shapes

Use soft rounded corners.

Buttons:

8–12px radius

Cards:

16–24px radius

Large containers:

24px radius

The interface should feel supportive and approachable rather than rigid.

---

# Components

## Buttons

Primary:

- Solid Keystone Blue
- White text
- Subtle hover lift

Secondary:

- White background
- Blue border
- Blue text

---

## Cards

Use:

- soft surface backgrounds
- subtle shadows
- generous padding

Cards should feel approachable and easy to scan.

---

## Input Fields

Use:

- light background
- soft borders
- large click targets

## Badges

# Experience Principle

Every screen should answer:

"Does this make families feel confident that their student will be supported here?"

If not, redesign it.
