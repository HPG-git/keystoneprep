Admissions Overview

A Personal, Thoughtful Approach to Every Application

At Keystone Prep, we want to learn about your child's academic background, strengths, challenges, goals, and support needs so we can determine whether Keystone Prep is the right fit. Every student's path is different, and this application helps us begin that conversation.

Before You Apply

Families are encouraged to gather any available educational records or assessments before submitting. These may include:

  • Psychoeducational evaluations
  • IEP or 504 plans
  • Recent report cards or transcripts
  • Therapy, tutoring, or support documentation
  • Any other information that helps us understand your student's needs

What Families Can Expect

Our admissions team reviews each application carefully and considers the student's academic needs, social-emotional development, learning profile, and readiness for Keystone Prep's project-based, student-centered environment.

We look for students who can benefit from small classes, individualized support, meaningful relationships with teachers, and real-world preparation for life after high school.

Who We Serve

Keystone Prep is designed for students who benefit from a smaller, more personalized high school environment — particularly students with average to above-average cognitive abilities who need support with areas such as attention, anxiety, learning differences, executive functioning, motivation, confidence, or prior negative school experiences.

Keystone Prep is not designed as a primary placement for students whose main needs are significant emotional or behavioral difficulties.

Admissions Process

What Happens After You Apply

Our process is straightforward and personal. Here's what to expect from submission through enrollment.

  1. 1
    Complete the application form below as fully as possible. If a question does not apply, you may write "N/A."
  2. 2
    Submit any available educational assessments or support documents — evaluations, IEPs, transcripts, or therapy records.
  3. 3
    The admissions team reviews the application and supporting materials.
  4. 4
    Families may be contacted to schedule a tour, interview, or student visit.
  5. 5
    The admissions team will notify families of the student's acceptance status.
  6. 6
    If accepted, families will receive a registration packet and enrollment next steps.
