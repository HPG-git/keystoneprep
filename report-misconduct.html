Reporting Educator Misconduct

All employees and agents of Keystone Prep High School have an obligation and legal responsibility to report misconduct by instructional personnel and school administrators which affects the health, safety, or welfare of a student.

Submit a Report

Failure to report misconduct may result in penalties up to termination of employment and revocation of an educator's certificate.

What to Report

Misconduct That Must Be Reported

Any behavior by instructional personnel or school administrators that affects the health, safety, or welfare of a student must be reported immediately. Examples of reportable misconduct include:

Reportable Behaviors
  • Obscene language
  • Drug and alcohol use
  • Disparaging comments
  • Prejudice or bigotry
  • Sexual innuendo
  • Cheating or testing violation
  • Physical aggression
  • Accept or offer favors

Important: Failure to report misconduct may result in penalties up to termination of employment and revocation of an educator's certificate under Florida law.
