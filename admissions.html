Our Approach

More Than Enrollment. It Starts With Understanding Your Student.

It is of utmost importance to determine if Keystone Prep is the correct placement for your student. To that end our Admissions team takes significant time and pays careful attention to the Psych-Ed Evaluation, IEP, EP, and/or 504 Plan that you provide. Since adolescence is a period in your students life whereby your student experiences significant and frequent changes, submitted Psych-Ed Evaluations should be no more than 2 years old. To ensure proper placement we require the most relevant and up to date information on your student.

Ultimately admissions is about making sure that we are the best fit for your student and that your student will fit well with our current students and staff. Once enrolled, new students become part of our Keystone Prep family and we go to great lengths to ensure that all family members will work peacefully together with the aim of a flourishing school environment for everyone.

Who We Serve

Students Who Often Thrive at Keystone

Keystone Prep supports a wide range of learners through individualized attention, smaller classes, and engaging learning experiences.

Students experiencing anxiety and/or depression

Students with Dyslexia, Dysgraphia, Dyscalculia, and Dyspraxia

Students with ADHD/ADD and/or Executive Functioning Challenges

Students present with a Level I Autism diagnosis on a case-by-case basis

Students seeking more individualized support

Gifted and Twice Exceptional students

Students who learn best through hands-on learning experiences and who will benefit from small classroom environments

Students rebuilding confidence in learning

Students who may feel overlooked in larger school settings
