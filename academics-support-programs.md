# Academic Support, Tracks & Graduation — Keystone Prep High School

---

## Ability-Targeted Academic Tracks

### Advanced Coursework

Many courses at Keystone Prep are offered at an **Honors level**. KPHS also has dual enrollment agreements with:

- Hillsborough Community College
- Pasco-Hernando Community College
- University of South Florida

These courses are appropriate for the general population of students, students with or without learning differences, and gifted students. Additionally, Keystone Prep High School is a **testing site for SAT, ACT**, and others.

---

### Modified Reading and Math — Access Points

KPHS offers modified courses for students needing additional support in Reading or Mathematics, following **Florida Access Points standards**. Key features:

- Students enrolled in modified courses may still choose a magnet track for elective courses
- This is a hybrid program where modifications to the curriculum in academic areas of need are administered by the ESE team

---

### General Education Coursework

Most courses fall under the General Education umbrella. These courses are appropriate for:

- The general student population
- Students with or without learning disabilities
- Gifted students

Note: General education courses are not modified for students with intellectual disabilities. Students with accommodations can access these courses.

---

## Accommodations

KPHS provides specific accommodations based on each student's unique needs. Staff uses IEPs, 504s, and psychoeducational evaluations to determine how accommodations should be administered and applied in the classroom.

Built into every classroom by default:

- Small class size
- Extended time
- Preferential seating
- Variety of assessment formats

---

## Florida Virtual School (FLVS)

Keystone Prep has partnered with Florida Virtual School to expand elective offerings, including:

- Driver's Education
- American Sign Language

An FLVS Coordinator works in direct contact with the KPHS facilitator to monitor student progress. This program provides students with additional support and reinforcement in an independent learning environment.

**Enrollment info:** [FLVS Flex Program](https://www.flvs.net/how-it-works/enrollment-options/flex/)

---

## Criteria to Graduate

KPHS offers the **Florida Department of Education 24-course requirement diploma**. Within these requirements:

- Honors credit is available
- Dual Enrollment options through HCC, Pasco-Hernando, and USF
- Preparation for **Bright Futures scholarship**
- Credit recovery when necessary
- Access Point courses for Reading and Mathematics
- Ability to extend beyond four years if needed
- Option to accelerate and graduate early

---

## Tutoring & Additional Support

When a student requires additional support, KPHS provides multiple layers of assistance:

1. **Teachers** — First line of support; dedicated daily time available for students in need
2. **Guidance Department** — Available for extra academic support
3. **Hillsborough County Public Schools Partnership** — For students who may qualify for:
   - Title I tutoring
   - Support services through the Parentally Placed Private School Students (PPPSS) program
